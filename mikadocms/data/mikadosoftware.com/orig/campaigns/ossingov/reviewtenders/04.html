Kent County Council (the Council) is the largest local authority in England covering an area of 3,500 square kilometres.  It has an annual expenditure of over £1bn on goods and services and a population of 1.3m.  The Council provides a wide range of personal and strategic services on behalf of its residents, operating in partnership with 12 district councils and 289 parish/town councils. 


 


Kent Teach


Kent Teach (www.kent-teach.com) has been developed for KCC. To enable schools in Kent to advertise vacancies and for the county’s Teacher Recruitment and Retention Team to advertise Kent, as an ideal place to work.


 


The website also enables costs to be reduced for recruitment for Kent schools. This is in line with Kent County Councils Bold Steps for education to ensure all schools are able to procure support services through EduKent*. The system also acts as a database as it enables schools to register newly qualified teachers and monitor their progress.


* https://shareweb.kent.gov.uk/Documents/jobs/els-bold-steps.pdf


The contract will be for a period of two years starting May 2013, with a further two one year extensions at the Council’s discretion.


Whilst KCC own the software and IPR’s, since 2000 the support maintenance and development of the site is currently outsourced and the contract now needs to be renewed.


 


The existing service provision needs to be maintained as it is the platform for educational employment in Kent. The Council expects that the use of the latest technologies will enhance and improve the system functionality, improve system reliability, reduce the cost of ongoing maintenance, provide a stable platform for future enhancement, development and growth and, as far as is possible, use non-proprietary and open source software in support of the system.


 


The Council expects to undertake quarterly service review meetings with the Supplier, which will allow feedback and improvement to be made to the website.

Assessment of Tenders


 


All tenders received will be considered on the information contained in the tender or obtained by the Council as a direct result of the tender process. Submissions will be assessed on the basis of most economically advantageous offer which will take account of the Contractor’s proven expertise and experience in the area of the Service for which he is tendering.


 


Tenders will be evaluated and weighted to the following criteria:


 


•        Technical Questions - Weighting at 60%


•        Pricing - Weighting at 40%


 


 


Technical Questions and Evaluation & Scoring


The assessment methodology which will be used to score price will be as follows:


Technical Questions (60%)


The assessment methodology which will be used to assess the answers to the technical questions will be as follows:


0 Marks – No Answer


1 Mark – Meets some of the requirement


2 Marks - Meets the requirement and responds to some of the projects key deliverables


3 Marks - Meets the requirement and meets the all of the projects key deliverables


4 Marks - Meets the above and shows sustainability of the support maintenance and development to the website providing added value to the bid


5 Marks - Meets the above and shows innovation and potential savings and efficiencies, providing added value to the bid. 




Pricing Evaluation and Scoring (40%)


Pricing will marked out of a total of 20 marks


The assessment methodology which will be used to score price will be as follows:


The lowest overall price will receive the maximum score, the next lowest price receives a percentage of the highest score.


For Example


Bid A £5,000 scores max. 20


Bid B £5,500 (500/5000x 100) (10% of 20 marks) scores 18


Bid C £6,500 (1500 / 5000 x 100) (30% of 20) scores 14


Bid D - £7,500 (2500/5000 x 100) (50% of 20) scores 10


 


The percentage of Rebate will be marked out of 20 marks. 


The bidder who bids the highest percentage of royalties will receive 20 marks


The bidder who bids the 2nd highest percentage of royalties will receive 15 marks


The bidder who bids the 3rd highest percentage of royalties will receive 10 marks


All other bidders will receive 5 marks.


 


The scores for the rebate and the pricing will be added together to calculate the mean score, which will be allocated to the supplier.
