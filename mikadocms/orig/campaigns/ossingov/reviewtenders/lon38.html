London Borough of Croydon (LBC) is seeking to engage a Service Delivery Partner (SDP) for the provision of its ICT services. This is an opportunity, both for Croydon and for the Partner, to deliver a flexible, effective and innovative ICT service that will support and enhance Croydon’s services to its customers and facilitate collaborative working with partners, whilst simultaneously delivering efficiency savings to the Council.


It is possible that other contracting authorities including, but not necessarily limited to, the police, NHS and any other relevant bodies within the South Outer London geographical area may wish to be recipients of some or all of the services to be provided under or pursuant to the contract.


Strategic Objectives for the Partnership


LBC’s objectives are to engage a SDP who will deliver:


·Value for Money:
To deliver services and associated flexibility that represent good value for the Council with an appropriately balanced approach to cost and quality – whilst improved services are core to the objectives of this procurement, so too are the delivery of efficiency savings;


·Flexibility:
To be able to flex contracts and approach to reflect both the changing nature/scope of the Council over the contract duration and developments in the ICT environment;


·Agility:
The ability of the ICT suppliers to be nimble and focused in meeting the changing needs of the council;


·Managed Risk:
To manage risk appropriately over the lifetime of the contract and enable innovation and change to flourish without losing control over service quality;


·Innovation:
To provide support, flexibility, knowledge and skills that enable the council to innovate and explore alternative technologies and ways of working;


·Working Anywhere:
Th e provision of ICT services, processes and devices that will enable council employees to work flexibly and from anywhere.


Scope – Overview


Croydon Council’s ICT services are largely provided by two external ICT suppliers and an internal ICT team inside the Council. The Council is seeking to increase the volume of ICT services it outsources to the SDP. The expected in-scope Service Components, out of scope Service Components and optional Service Components are detailed below.


Service Components In-scope


It is expected that all of the following areas will be delivered by the SDP directly.


Transition to the new SDP


The SDP will manage and execute the transition of ICT services from the existing ICT suppliers and internal ICT Team to their solution. A 9-month transition window is planned. The SDP will also be responsible for the transfer of any relevant knowledge and resources.


N.B. It is anticipated that a separate Data Centre and Disaster Recovery contract will be let before the SDP contract; and these services will be in place prior to the SDP contract commencement.


The Council will require a SDP who can rapidly mobilise to support this transition.


Service Provision


The SDP will be responsible for directly providing Service Delivery of the Council’s IT services. The core ITIL v3.0 elements of Service Provision are:


·Service Design: The Supplier will be expected to proactively manage the ICT Service provided to the Council. The Service will be structured in line with standard Information Technology Infrastructure Library (ITIL) v3.0 IT Service Categories and include proactive Service Management activities such as Capacity, Availability, Continuity, Security, Supplier Management, Service Level and Service Catalogue Management.


·Service Transition: The supplier will be responsible for Application Maintenance and Development services. The Service will be structured in line with standard ITIL v3.0 IT Service Categories and will include: Change Management; Service, Asset and Configuration Management; and Release and Deployment Management.


·Service Operation: The supplier will be expected to run the day-to-day operation of the service and the interface with the Council. The Service will be structured in line with standard ITIL v3.0 IT Service Categories and will include Request Fulfilment, Incident Management, Event Management and Access Management.


·Continual Service Improvement: The Supplier will be expected to enhance the service throughout the life of the contract. The Service will be structured in line with standard ITIL v3.0 IT Service Categories and will include Service Reporting, Return on Investment (ROI), Service Measurement and Service Improvement Plus.


Service Towers


The SDP will be responsible for the Service Towers.  The Service Towers include end user devices (e.g. Desktop, laptop, tablet, etc.), Local Area Network, Unified Communications, Security Infrastructure, ICT Infrastructure, Infrastructure Applications, Commercial Off-the-Shelf (COTS) applications, and Line of Business applications.


The Council anticipates a number of specific Service Towers, identified below, being provided through a supply chain in order to meet specialist delivery requirements and encourage innovation, as well as enabling flexibility in contracting for commoditised hardware and services. These Service Towers are:


· Managed Print;


· Mobile Communications;


· IT Hardware and Software procurement;


· External Network Services, and


· Contact Centre Technology.


The Council anticipates that the SDP will create its own supply chain for the delivery of these towers.


Professional Services


The Council currently expects that these will be procured through an existing Council framework, however the Council reserves the right to access these services from the SDP where that makes business sense. These services could include but not be limited to: Business and Technology transformation; programme and project management, and significant change management and business analysis.


Service Components Out of Scope


There are two areas which are outside of the scope of the contract for the SDP and will be procured separately and directly by the Council:


· Data Centre and Disaster Recovery:
This is expected to be the subject of a single restricted procurement. Any resulting contract will contain break points that are explicitly out of alignment with the Prime agent Contract to mitigate future transition risk. However, the resulting contract will be managed by the SDP;


· Web Design and Hosting:
This is expected to be the subject of a separate framework agreement to allow the engagement of multiple suppliers supporting access innovative solutions and small and medium-sized enterprises (SME’s) in the market place. The resulting contract will be directly managed by the Council.


Optional Service Components


The following Service Components may be included within this procurement at the option of the Council for the purposes of ensuring suitable provision for contingencies and flexibility. These include:


1. In -house ICT service delivery:
This includes elements of the retained function including: Application Support and Training; Systems Development; ICT support to Schools currently delivered via CrEdIT; and Services currently provided by CALAT to Adult Education.


2. Finance, HR and Payroll:
Whilst the Council is currently pursuing provision of these services through other routes, it may choose to bring them in scope at some future date or to procure them independently.


3. Revenue & Benefits Hosting and Printing specialist:
Whilst the Council is currently pursuing provision of these services through other routes, they may choose to include them within the scope of this contract at some future date or to procure them independently.


4. LBC Library service ICT:
Whilst the Council is currently pursuing provision of these services through other routes, they may choose to include them within the scope of this contract at some future date or to procure them independently.

Total quantity or scope

The contract is expected to be for an eight (8) year period with an option (on the part of the contracting authority) to terminate the contract for convenience with effect from the end of years five (5) and seven (7). Additionally there will be an option on the part of the contracting authority to extend the contract by a further period of one (1) year with effect from the end of the eight (8) year initial contract period.

Estimated Value: The expected aggregate value of this contract for provision of services to the Council is £250m (based on an eight (8) year projected contract term).
