Summary of Current Position


The Customer Service Strategy, launched in January 2012, seeks to improve customer experience for Kent County Councilâ€™s customers whilst reducing the cost to serve. The strategy focuses on making things simpler, easier and more straightforward, for example by increasing self-service through electronic tools. 


The council recognises that it has a very diverse customer base and therefore expecting all customers to access all services online through a website is inappropriate and unrealistic.


The council therefore wants to test take up of additional electronic means of reporting issues and requesting services, through digital TV, and through mobile phone based applications.


The council therefore wishes to appoint an established software supplier for a pilot period of two years to demonstrate and evaluate the potential of delivering services through these routes.


The reason behind a 2 year pilot is to fully test the customer acceptance and savings potential of a tool that supports digital self service across several platforms.  This will include a full exploration of the integration potential of the tool with existing and expected technical architecture.


Specification


The Council is seeking a company to provide software tools to allow it to communicate with citizens. The software tool is required to do the following:



  • Provision of a digital TV platform to provide browsable council information and allow reporting/requesting of services

  • Provision of a smartphone app (to include iOS and Android) to provide browsable council information and allow reporting/requesting of services

  • Both platforms should be able to be managed by a single content management system which allows variation of content by channel & service

  • Both platforms should support citizen self-service and include services such as but not limited to 

    • Highways Faults

    • Applying for Free School Meals

    • Applying for Home to School Transport

    • Self-Management of Social Care needs


  • The solution should provide APIs to allow it to integrate with MS Dynamics CRM

  • The appointed supplier will work with the council to ensure that notifications received through these tools can be tailored to our business requirements

  • A management information function allowing design of reports to include but not limited to numbers of downloads of App, Total number of reports/requests received, number of reports/requests abandoned, reports/requests abandoned point of abandon   


Expected outputs



  • A KCC Digital TV platform to enable citizens to browse information and report a number of issues/request services

  • A KCC Smartphone App to browse information and report a number of issues/request services


Timescales


The Council requires the work to be carried out from April 2013. This initial pilot is for two years and a separate market engagement and procurement exercise will be carried out after December 2014 for April 2015 onwards. 


 Assessment Methodology


Tenderers will be required to provide or demonstrate the following;



  1. Provide case study evidence and references of where the company has already provided a similar service to other Local Government authorities including identified savings and timescales for achieving those savings

  2. Provide an implementation plan outlining how the work will be approached; including any resources they will be using and proposed timescales

  3. Detailed Description of how the proposed solution meets the councils needs and can support a channel shift programme

  4. Provide evidence, including Curriculum Vitae, of the experience of the staff who will be carrying out the work

  5. Set out what resourcing/participation they will require from the Council to undertake the work set out in the specification and proposed timescales


Interview


Tenderers may be asked to attend an interview to discuss their bid and answer any additional questions the council may have in relation to the bid.


The Purpose of this Notice


We are conducting a consultation exercise to give organisations who might want to tender, the opportunity of commenting on the draft specification.  The outcome of the market engagement will help determine how the council structures its tender opportunity in order to maximise effectiveness and value for money.


To this end, all suppliers that express an interest will be invited to comment.  This is a two week consultation exercise and comments must be submitted by noon on Friday 29th MARCH 2013.
